## Design Context

### Users
Small and mid-market business owners in the US who need a bilingual (English/Spanish) web presence. Primary: Spanish-speaking owners of small businesses in the US who want a site that serves both their English and Spanish-speaking customer base. Secondary: mid-market US companies looking for a bilingual consultancy. They arrive researching, compare a few options, and decide in minutes.

### Job to Be Done
The site must sell on its own — generate qualified inbound leads so Luis doesn't have to do active sales. Visitors need to trust him fast, understand the offering in under 30 seconds, and feel that the quality of the site reflects the quality of the work they'll receive.

### Brand Personality
Credible, bilingual, premium, modern. The bilingual aspect is not a feature bolt-on — it's the core differentiator and should feel native to the design, not like a toggle stuck on top of an English-first product.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Inbound-focused consultancy site: calm, confident, specific.
- Positions Luis as a trusted specialist, not an anonymous agency.
- Leans premium without being corporate. Modern without being trendy.
- Supports light and dark themes (existing next-themes integration).
- Avoid generic "AI agency" visual language (cyan-on-dark, purple gradients, glowing accents, hero metric template).

### Design Principles
1. **Trust before flash.** Every element should make the visitor feel more confident that Luis can deliver. No empty animations or decoration-for-decoration.
2. **Bilingual is structural, not optional.** EN and ES are equal citizens. Layouts must accommodate Spanish copy length (typically 15–30% longer) without breaking.
3. **Premium through restraint.** Fewer, better elements. Generous space. Sharp typography. Avoid stacking sections that all look the same.
4. **Specific over generic.** Concrete proof (real project, founder's face, clear pricing, direct claims) beats abstract marketing language.
5. **Inbound funnel is the point.** Every section should answer a question a prospect is asking; the path to "contact Luis" should always be close at hand.
